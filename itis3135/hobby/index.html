How to Make Pho

Pho is a delicious Vietnamese noodle soup that you can make at home. Here’s a simple recipe with ingredients and step-by-step instructions:

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef bones (for broth)
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 1 lb beef (thinly sliced)
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil, mint)
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced chili peppers (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth (2 hours): Boil the beef bones, then simmer with onion, ginger, cinnamon, star anise, fish sauce, and sugar for 2 hours.
  2. Cook the noodles (10 minutes): Soak and cook the rice noodles according to package instructions.
  3. Assemble the Pho (5 minutes): Place cooked noodles in a bowl, add thinly sliced beef, and pour hot broth over it to cook the beef.
  4. Garnish (5 minutes): Top with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime, and optional sliced chili peppers.

Total Time Estimate: Approximately 2 hours and 20 minutes

Why I Love Pho

Pho has always held a special place in my heart, not just as a dish, but as a symbol of family and togetherness.

Growing up, my mother would often make Pho for our family. The aroma of simmering broth would fill the kitchen, creating a warm and comforting atmosphere. It was a meal that brought us all together at the dinner table, where we shared stories and laughter. To this day, the taste of Pho reminds me of the love and care my mother put into every bowl she made.

Beyond its connection to my mother, Pho has also become a way to bond with friends and family. Whether it’s gathering for a meal at a favorite Pho restaurant or making a big pot of Pho at home, it’s always a communal experience. The process of preparing and enjoying Pho together creates meaningful memories and strengthens relationships.
